Consider this analogy. Say I like chocolate ice-cream , and think it's far superior to other flavours and it becomes part of my identity. I want others to like it as well, and am critical of others who like different flavors of it.
Letting go of my attachment would mean accepting that others flavours of ice-cream are equally fine - it's just a matter of choice. I would continue to enjoy chocolate ice-cream, but now be able to make close friendships with people who liked other flavours. I would certainly never get angry with or kill anyone who said they hated chocolate ice-cream. Occasionally, I may even try other flavours to see why people liked them. In real life I may still call myself American, Democrat, Christian, Vegan and so on - but completely let go of my attachment to these identities and be able to connect deeply with others who may be different.
