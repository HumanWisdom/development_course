Have you noticed how in a conversation people are so much interested in talking about themselves and telling their stories, rather than in listening to others? Our self-interest operates in the background, and impacts how we communicate. We scan a conversation for something interesting and stop listening if we are not being stimulated. We get pleasure from talking about ourselves, so steer the conversation to do that. We have a subtle need for importance so may try to show we are better than others in some way. Or we may approach a person thinking ‘What's in it for me’?

This is not right or wrong, but just how the human mind functions. This can help us understand others better, and allow us to put our self-interest to one side to build connections with others.
