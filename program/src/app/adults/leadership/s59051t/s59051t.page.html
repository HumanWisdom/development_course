What stops us delegating effectively to others? Or, what makes us want to do everything ourselves? Good leadership is about effective delegation. How can we do that? We may have a particular way we want things done, and think others should do it our way. When we delegate we have to accept people will have their own way of doing things, and as long as it achieves the same result, it should be broadly okay, unless there are ethical issues involved. Our fear of things not getting done can make us want to micro-manage situations, and that can irritate people. The challenge in delegating authority is that we are not delegating responsibility. We are still responsible for what happens.
