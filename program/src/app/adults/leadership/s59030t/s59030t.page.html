There is a crisis in leadership the world over. Finding good leaders is becoming harder. 1 in 2 people will leave a job in their careers just to get away from a bad boss. A shocking 70% of employees in the US are unhappy because of negative management, and this is likely to be the same the world over. Being a leader is a tough, lonely job, and few people are equipped with the inner resilience to cope and burnout rates are high. Because many leaders lack empathy and people skills, workplace bullying is common. A third of leaders fail at their jobs. What this translates into are millions of unhappy employees who find work stressful and an unhappy place to be, and distressed leaders who struggle to cope. This drains productivity. When it comes to government the impact of poor leadership is seen on a much larger scale. It does not have to be this way.
