We have explored how our hidden self-interest operates behind the scenes directing our thoughts and actions. This is not wrong, and just part of being human. What challenges can it pose in organisations, especially when people are not aware of it? It can hinder cooperation between people if one person feels their self-interest is threatened - sharing some work in the fear the other person will take credit for it, for example. It can set up unnecessary rivalries between departments vying for attention or resources, within the same organisation. We can see that clearly in politics, with different factions competing for power. People can put others down in order to get ahead themselves. Once you start noticing it, it is everywhere. It just needs to be understood.
