There are times in life when there are many factors to consider when making a decision. Each of these is pulling you in a different direction but you can make only one decision. How do you decide?

There is no easy answer but writing everything down is a good first step. Then consider how important each factor is for you and assign it a value from 0-5, and then put them in two columns and see how they add up.

For example if you are thinking of dating someone for a long term relationship there will be many things to consider: Looks, compatibility, kindness, sense of humour, secure income, the views of your friends and family, shared interests, ability to communicate, flexibility, how you feel, and so on.

If you think of looks you may want to be with that person, but if you think of shared interests you don't. You feel torn, unsure of what to do.

Will you use pure logic and be sensible, or follow your emotions, or a blend of both?

This is where talking to others can help - to help you figure out what is most important for you, and base your decision on that
