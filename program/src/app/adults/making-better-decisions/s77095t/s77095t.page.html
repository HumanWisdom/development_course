We often make decisions on incomplete information and that makes for bad decisions. It is worth pausing to ask if you have all the facts to hand before deciding.

For example, when buying a house have you considered what the neighbours are like, or when deciding whom to marry have you spent enough time getting to know the other person?

We also make assumptions about people - for example in a doctors surgery when you see the same patient repeatedly it is easy to assume that it is the same problem they are presenting with each time - but that may not be the case.
