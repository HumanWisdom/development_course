For decades it was thought depression was caused by a chemical imbalance in the brain, of a chemical called serotonin. This led to a new class of drugs called serotonin-uptake inhibitors (SSRI’s) being prescribed. Common examples include Prozac, Citalopram or Sertraline. In 2022 two scientists from University College London evaluated all the research so far and concluded that there was no evidence that depression was linked to a chemical imbalance in the brain, and the SSRI’s were no better than a placebo in their effect. A placebo effect simply means if you believe a treatment is going to work, in most cases it does
