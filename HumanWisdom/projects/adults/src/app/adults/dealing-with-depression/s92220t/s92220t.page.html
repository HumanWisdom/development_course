Our mind makes assumptions of ourselves and others and we automatically attach a label.

We may believe we are useless, worthless or will never work again. Or we may think we are unattractive.

We also make assumptions of other people in our life - usually looking at them negatively - and thinking the worst of them - people are selfish, uncaring, or mean, for example.

Our assumptions are also binary - we label ourselves and others as good or bad, right or wrong - not realising that each person is a mix of many shades of gray - we are just human after all.

We repeat these assumptions and so they stick and we never question them. They keep us anchored in old ways of thinking that perpetuate our depression
