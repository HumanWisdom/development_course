1 in 7 women will experience depression after childbirth - it's called post-partum depression and occurs usually within 6 weeks of giving birth.

It can also occur after a challenging life event - like bereavement, or failure of a business or the loss of a relationship, or bullying.

Childhood trauma and neglect can manifest years later and cause depression.

It can also be caused by substance abuse - alcohol or drugs.

It can be the result of long-term, chronic stress.

Depression can be a side-effect of taking some medicines, and can also be triggered by several medical conditions like a diagnosis of cancer.

It is known to run in families. If you have a family member with depression you are at a higher risk of suffering it yourself.

If you have certain personality traits like low self-esteem or are too critical of yourself you are more prone to depression.
