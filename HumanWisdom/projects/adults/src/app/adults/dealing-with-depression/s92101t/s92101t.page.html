There are many reasons why we may struggle to accept that we have a mental illness like depression.

It may be that we have good careers, feel strong and do not think this could happen to us. Depression happens to ‘other people’, not us.

We may feel we are managing our day to day lives, and everyone feels blue sometimes and we are no different . We don’t want to make a fuss and worry others.

Or, we may not like having the label depressed - even for ourselves. We like to think of ourselves as healthy - and do not like labels.

Or, we may worry what other people will think of us - especially at work and how our loved ones will react. Will they think less of us? Will they still support us and stay in our life, or leave us?

We may be dealing with a major life event like divorce, bereavement or the loss of a job, and we are sure how we are feeling is related to the external event, and will soon pass.

Or, we are so overwhelmed with the emotions we just want to escape from them and numb the pain we feel with alcohol or drugs
