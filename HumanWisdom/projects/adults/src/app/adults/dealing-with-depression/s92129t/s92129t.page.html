When we are depressed we feel low and our mind is full of negative thoughts about our current state, our disappointments and feeling a lack of hope. We are caught in a loop of our own thinking.

In this situation it is useful to ground ourselves into the present and havening is a technique invented by Neuroscientist Dr Ronald Ruden. It involves a distinctive self-soothing motion with crossed arms, gently but noticeably stroking from shoulders to elbows. Its aim is to create a feeling of well-being and safety.
