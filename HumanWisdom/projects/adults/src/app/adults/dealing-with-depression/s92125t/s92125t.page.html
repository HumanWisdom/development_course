Now let’s introduce another element to this exercise.

Try this only if you have mastered step 1 and 2 and are comfortable with it. This third step is not always easy - so try it - and if it doesn’t feel comfortable right now, come back to it later.

As you breathe in, at the end of your breath, hold it for a count of 2 before you begin to breathe out. As you breathe out, at the end of your breath hold it to a count of 2 before you breathe in again.

As you get better at this increase the breath holding count to 4.

Being able to control your breath in this way is good for your immune system, and also helps you to control your automatic reactions to your thoughts or external events.

Do this for 5 minutes
