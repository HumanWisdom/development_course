Loneliness is at the heart of being human and up to 60% of people feel lonely, 30% of them in marriages. We think having lots of friends on social media is an answer, and maybe it distracts us from that feeling for a while, but it soon returns. It is a paradox that loneliness is more common among young people, who are the most digitally connected generation. It is the depth of connection we have with another person that determines how lonely we feel, and social media is not a natural place for that. We have not been taught the communication skills we need to deepen our connection with others. We join groups and have lots of online friends but do not have the real life connections that we need to not feel lonely.
