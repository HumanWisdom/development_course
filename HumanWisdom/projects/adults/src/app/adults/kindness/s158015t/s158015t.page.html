Let me share two stories of people who were kind to me, and the impact that had on me. The first was about 40 years ago when I was a medical student, attending a wedding in Bombay. My uncle got chest pain, and was having a heart attack. We got him into a taxi and went to the nearest hospital. On the way his heart stopped working and we revived him. At the hospital he was rushed into the Intensive Care Unit - but we were told there were no beds for him. I remember a young doctor who reassured us and arranged for him to stay on the trolley, in the middle of the ICU, and kept looking after him, till a bed was found elsewhere. He went out of his way to be kind, and I never forgot that. The second was when my son was 2 years old and admitted for some surgery to his nose. It was under general anesthetic. The procedure was simple but as a parent I was so worried about him. After the procedure the surgeon called me to say everything had gone well. He need not have done that - and its a kindness I never forgot. My father was a doctor for 50 years and he used to say - the only thing your patients will remember is your kindness. I practiced that myself, and passed that on to all the young surgeons I trained in my career as a spine surgeon.
