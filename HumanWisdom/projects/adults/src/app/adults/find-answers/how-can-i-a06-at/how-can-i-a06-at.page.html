Feel good about myself

If you don’t feel good about who you are, you are not alone. It is a common feeling, and one that many of us experience at some point in our lives. The good news is that like most feelings it will not last forever, but when you are experiencing it it can feel particularly distressing. You can get into a loop of negative thinking and you can convince yourself what you think is true.

Have you ever wondered what is going on in your mind to make you feel this way? You need curiosity to observe your own thinking, and to be able to question what you find.

Looking and learning about our own mind offers long term freedom from this feeling.

The first thing you can notice is that our mind is always looking for external validation - we need others to like us before we can like ourselves. We are not aware of this process in ourselves. When we become aware of it you realise it does not serve us - people are too busy thinking about themselves to think about us - we need to learn to value ourselves and be comfortable being who we are.

You can also see that our mind creates certain ideals of the person we should be - we should have more friends, or be more successful, or more well known, or have more followers on social media. When we don’t match those ideals we can feel bad about ourselves. When we wake up and realise that these ideas of who we should be are a creation of our own thinking and part of our conditioning influences we can let them go - and relax into being the human being that we are.

This deeper understanding of our own mind brings peace, so we can enjoy the gift of every day and truly celebrate the life we have.
