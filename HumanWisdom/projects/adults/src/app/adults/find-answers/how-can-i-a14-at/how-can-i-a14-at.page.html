Be a better leader

Leading others is both a privilege and an opportunity to make a difference. Our mind can mistake that privilege as an opportunity to boost our own ego and wield power over others.

To lead other people you need to begin by being able to lead yourself - to be able to manage your own emotions, habits and mental health. Leadership is not a skill we are born with, but one that we can all learn.

Leadership is about understanding people so you can get the best out of them. The more you understand yourself, the easier it is to understand others.

To lead others we need to get them to sign up to a shared vision of where you, as a leader, want to go, and inspire them to make it their vision as well. You then look after them as they work towards it.

Leadership is not about using power and fear as a tool to get things done, even though you may be tempted to do so. No one gives their best in an atmosphere of fear. This does not mean that people are not accountable though.

The list of qualities leaders need to succeed is long - integrity, self-awareness, emotional intelligence, resilience, relationship building, making good decisions, staying calm in a storm.

As a leader you will make several important decisions. If you understand the hidden emotional drivers in your own mind that influence the decisions you make, you can make better ones, for yourself, and the people you serve.
