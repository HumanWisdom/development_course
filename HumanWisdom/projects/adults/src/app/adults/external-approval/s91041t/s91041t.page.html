This hidden need we have for Need for approval can influence our behaviour and can make us choose options in life where we will get praised or recognised. If I have been praised as a child for my academic achievements I can grow up seeking that from my work and my relationships – and if I don’t get it, I can get frustrated. We can manipulate others unknowingly – ‘How do I look’ is a common question, or say ‘I love you’ in the hope of having it said back to us.

If I am a surgeon I may try and do a complex procedure rather than a simpler one, in the hope of getting recognition from my colleagues. Or, dedicate myself to writing research papers even though I hate research – for the same reason.

When studying, we might not actually enjoy the subject, but focus on the exams and the validation that may bring. Or as a musician, focus on the praise and recognition my performing brings, and stop enjoying playing the instrument for its own sake.

Or we may flatter others to ‘butter them up’ in the hope of getting something from them.
