Any strong emotion, like stress, anxiety or anger can cloud our judgment and influence our decision making.

If we have suffered from trauma in the past we can be easily triggered to anger or stress and make decisions that are not always in our best interest - like drinking too much for example.

When our emotions are running high we can make decisions without considering all the options available to us and often make the wrong ones - a sort of knee jerk reaction. Politicians do this in reaction to an adverse newspaper report for example.

Or if we are angry we may decide to leave a relationship or hit someone - all of which can impact our future.

It is common sense to postpone any decisions to when we are feeling more calm.
