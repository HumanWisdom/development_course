There are always other ways of looking at a situation - but our mind usually jumps to conclusions - for example, if you are called to see the boss in his office, our mind assumes there is a problem, when in fact there may not be.

Or at work if you are faced with a problem it is worth stepping back and exploring other, less obvious ways of looking at the situation. This could avoid so many medical errors - for example a patient may present with back pain and the doctor may forget to consider a diagnosis of cancer, and that could delay the diagnosis for the patient.

Or, if a child on a day trip is not reachable, rather than panic, it is possible the phone ran out of battery, and so on.

If is therefore worth asking - what assumptions am I making here, and can I re-examine them?
