How to deal with an illness

The distress caused to a patient by an illness, no matter whether it is severe back pain, cancer, or by a heart attack is a combination of two things - the illness itself and the pain and disability it causes, and our emotional reaction to it.

Sometimes the emotional reaction to the illness can be far more than the actual suffering it causes - e.g a diagnosis of breast cancer. Modern medicine is great at treating the cause of illness itself, but not so good at treating the emotional distress it causes.

This emotional distress is felt not just by the patient but by the entire family.

As a result of a diagnosis of cancer for example, we can experience a shower of thoughts and emotions and struggle to deal with them all. There could be anger at what has happened, regret at perhaps not looking after our health, many fears for the future and for our loved ones, and so on.

How can we cope with them all?

The main thing is to try and calm the mind down so you can think rationally - do a breathing exercise, journal your thoughts down, start meditating, or do some tapping. All of these will help. Speak to your friends. Create a support group for yourself.

There is a journey to acceptance of what has happened and that acceptance will bring you peace - but it is a journey and can take time.
