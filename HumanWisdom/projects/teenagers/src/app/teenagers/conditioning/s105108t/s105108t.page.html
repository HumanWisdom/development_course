Our conditioning is also responsible for all our prejudices.
If we meet people who are different to us, it creates a disturbance in our thinking. That disturbance can make us feel threatened and challenge our assumptions of who we are. Our fear can make us respond with aggression. One way for our mind to feel less disturbed is for those who are causing us to feel that way to change - and that can lead to intolerance of people with different opinions, or different sexual identities, or want people to leave - in case of immigrants of a different skin color or national identity to our own.
This prejudice takes many forms like racism, homophobia, sexism, anti-semitism and islamophobia. We are also conditioned by the prejudices of the people around us and we absorb them without thinking. That is why some prejudices, like anti-semitism and homophobia get handed down from generation to generation.
