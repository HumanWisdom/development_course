Welcome.
In this series of awareness exercises we are going to learn a set of skills which can help us understand ourselves and how our mind works, and then apply this to different areas of our life. You do not need a degree to learn these skills - just as you do not need a degree to learn to cook, or to swim.

Just as our hearts work in the same way, so does our thinking. Understanding the way our heart works helped us to prevent and treat many problems linked to the heart. Understanding the way our mind works can help us do the same. This understanding also helps us to understand others better, think clearly, manage our own emotions, and make good decisions for our lives.

Benefits of understanding our own thinking

  • Help us think clearly
  • Be emotionally intelligent
  • Make good decisions
  • Understand others
  • Communicate better

These exercises are the key to developing the skills you need to enjoy your life and live with wisdom, rather than just read about it.

It is the difference between just reading a travel guide about London, and actually going to visit the city yourself.

Without self-knowledge you are not properly educated

J Krishnamurti

We are now going to invite you to a five day challenge.

Do one exercise a day and record what you are learning in your journal

These exercises are powerful and the key to living your best life

Day 1

See the world freshly

In this first set of exercises we are going to remind ourselves how to notice the world around us with depth and wonder and be curious about what we see.

You will notice things around you that you would normally overlook, and write about them in your journal. The aim is to improve your ability to notice. Our mind tends to overlook things it sees everyday. This skill will help you see the world freshly each day, and that in itself can be life enhancing.

This ability to notice freshly will be invaluable when you begin noticing your thoughts and feelings. We are going to begin with noticing the people we meet freshly

Look at everything as though you are seeing it for the first time, with the eyes of a child, fresh with wonder.

Joseph Cornell

Day 5

Walking with awareness

Normally when we walk our mind wanders. We take note of things around us only briefly - before the mind wanders off again. Walk consciously. Notice the air you breathe, the sound your footsteps make, and the details of the buildings or landscape around you.

The more you notice the quieter your mind becomes. Pause from time to time and look carefully at a tree or a flower or a building - noticing the details.

As you develop your ability to notice and be curious about the world around you, your sense of wonder at being alive will awaken

I have travelled all around the world to see rivers and mountains and cities, and spent a great deal of money. But I forgot to step outside my house and see a dewdrop on a blade of grass, a dewdrop which reflects in its convexity the whole universe.

Challenge yourself to walk consciously, noticing the air you breathe and the details of the world around you. Walk with wonder, be curious, and be grateful.
