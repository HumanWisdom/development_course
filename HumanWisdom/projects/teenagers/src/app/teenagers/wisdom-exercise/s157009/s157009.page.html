Exercise #8

Card exercises

In this series of exercises we explore practical situations you may encounter through a series of conversations and card exercises. Each day has a new video to watch, questions to answer in your journal and some practical take-aways. Keep in mind the basic principles of enquiry:

  • We are just looking and learning about ourselves
  • Have a mind that is curious and open to learning
  • For learning to take place we need to pause judgement
  • What we observe in ourselves is not right or wrong, good or bad
  • Deep down, we are all the same human beings

Introduction to these exercises

How do you respond to new information? With curiosity, or resistance?

We are just looking and learning about ourselves. Something is only true, if you see it clearly yourself.

Something seen clearly within ourselves brings illumination. Illumination brings transformation, often without effort.

Different points of view

Examples of different points of view

Learning:

  • Our mind assumes ours in the only point of view
  • We cannot understand why others don’t see things our way
  • When you encounter a different perspective - be curious
  • Understand that everyone has a different point of view

Differences of opinion

Learning:

  • We want others to agree with us
  • We try to convince them, and they try to convince us
  • We are attached to our own opinions and want to defend them
  • We ignore any facts that challenge our opinion
  • If you try to convince others, they just dig in some more

Most of all, differences of opinion are opportunities for learning.

Terry Williams

Where do our opinions come from, and Why are we so attached to them?

Learning:

  • Our opinions come from our past influences, but we are not aware of this
  • We think our opinions are originally ours
  • We get attached to our opinions because they become part of our identity
  • Attachment creates conflict, and blocks understanding of the truth

Where do our prejudices come from?

Learning:

  • We notice prejudices in others, but not in ourselves
  • They come from our past influences
  • We like to be with people who share our views
  • They stop us seeing people freshly and avoiding them
  • We do not like our prejudices to being challenged

Opinions formed from prejudice are always sustained with the greatest violence.

Anon

When you meet people who are different, be curious, not fearful. Get to know them. Sow a seed for peace in the world. Every little counts.

How do I see myself? How do others see me?

Learning:

  • We see ourselves through the lens of our conditioning
  • Each person sees us through the lens of their conditioning
  • How we see ourselves is often different to how others see us
  • Our opinions of ourselves, negative, or positive, may not be true

I see you not as you are, but as I am

Our anxiety of what others think of us

It is surprising that we all love ourselves more than other people, but care more about their opinions than our own.

Marcus Aurelius

Learning:

  • Our anxiety about what others thinks feels real, but may not be true
  • People may not be thinking about us at all
  • Just as we do not like everyone we meet, we cannot expect everyone to like us
  • If we need others to feel good about ourselves, we are going to be unhappy

If you can stop worrying what others think, or comparing yourself with them, you can be free to walk your own road in life and be happy.

Common problems in communication

The single biggest problem in communication is the illusion that it has taken place.

George Bernard Shaw

  • Communication problems are common
  • It is worth checking what the other person has understood
  • It is also worth asking a question to check what you have understood
  • Be sensitive to the feeling behind the words being spoken

The masks we wear

Learning:

  • We wear masks because we want to control what others think about us
  • We cannot face the reality of who we are, or show that to the world
  • Underneath our masks we can be unhappy, anxious, or feel we are a failure
  • Having the courage to be ourselves, allows others to love us for who we are
  • And helps us deal with whatever needs addressing, and be at peace

Do they love you or the mask you put on every day.

Shimika Bowers

If you wear a mask long enough you begin to forget who you are beneath it

Alan Moore

Have the courage to be authentic, to say what you mean, and mean what you say. Let your words reflect your truth. It is okay - you are just human like everyone else.

Exploring Identity. How does it form?

Learning:

  • We acquire our identities from our various influences
  • Then we get attached to them
  • We are not aware of this process
  • The identities divide us from others, and can cause conflict
  • We want to be with people like us. This is how tribes form.

Letting go of our attachment to our identities

The root of suffering is attachment.

Buddha

Benefits of letting go of our attachment to our identities:

  • Reduced conflict in our relationships
  • Connect with others as the human beings we are
  • Contribute to peace in the world
  • Feel a great inner freedom to just be you
