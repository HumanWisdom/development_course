Introducing

Noticing your body

Welcome to this session where we are going to remind ourselves how to become more sensitive to our own body, and all the different signals it is sending us.

We generally take our body for granted don’t we? As long as it is functioning normally and nothing is hurting we don’t pay it much attention.

These exercises are about noticing different aspects of our body - our breathing, our hunger and thirst, our posture, our walking, our habits, and the tension in our muscles.

This can help us eat healthily, drink enough water, exercise adequately, and spot the emotions we may be feeling much earlier, because they often manifest in our body first.

Noticing how we are breathing can lead to a calm mind

Benefits of being more sensitive to your body:

  • Eat healthily
  • Stay hydrated
  • Notice emotions early
  • Exercise adequately
  • Spot early warning signals of disease
  • Avoid obesity

Take care of your body. It is the only place you have to live.

Jim Rohn.

Day 1

The gift of breath. A simple breathing exercise.

The ability to breathe is a gift. Just try holding your breath for a minute and see how you feel. From ancient times the art of breathing has been regarded as crucial to health. The ancient Chinese had a book with 7 volumes dedicated to it. Pranayama is the ancient science of breathing from India.

Try this exercise. Wherever you are, focus on your breath and take a deep breath in through your nose counting silently to 4, hold it to a count of 2, then release it slowly to a count of six. Let's do that one more time together - take a deep breath in counting to 4, hold it to a count of 2 and release the breath to a count of 6 slowly. Do this throughout the day whenever you have a few minutes spare.

The deep inhalation provides vital oxygen to the tissues. Retention steadies the mind and the slow smooth release of the breath relaxes the mind

Conscious breathing is a simple way to calm your mind and connect your mind and body. It allows you to connect to your surroundings. See the breathing exercise module for more.

Conscious breathing heightens awareness and deepens relaxation.

Dan Brule

Day 2

The benefits of eating and drinking with awareness

Eating and drinking consciously based on what our body is telling us allows us to be healthy, avoid obesity, and stay well hydrated.

Many diseases like diabetes are linked to people being overweight. Obesity rates are rising, and are 42% in the US and 28% in the UK. When we eat we are usually thinking of something else. If we lived with a greater awareness of what we were eating, and what the body was signaling, this problem could be avoided.

Eating and drinking for pleasure is not wrong, but can contribute to obesity. Alcohol is a toxin which has been linked to several diseases including cancer.

Scientists are also finding links between the type of food we eat and disease. Colon cancer for example is linked to eating too much red meat.

Eat to fuel your body, not to feed your emotions.

Anon

Eating and drinking consciously, with awareness, allows us to eat in moderation, enjoy what we eat, and be healthier.

Today, eat and drink with a deeper awareness of what is happening in your mind as you do so, and notice what your body is signaling to you.

The simple act of noticing how we sit and stand can help develop good posture, and help us become more aware of our body.

Day 4

Try conscious walking

When we walk we are usually thinking of something else.

Try walking consciously, with a back that is straight, the head in line with the shoulders and the eyes looking at the horizon. `Do this with an awareness of your breath, breathing slowly and deeply.

Walk tall, without slouching.

Think of it as a kind of meditation. Be aware of your posture, the movement of your arms and legs, your balance, and your breathing. Notice how wonderful this feels

Walking brings me back to myself

Laurette Mortimer

Day 6

Notice how your emotions are felt in your body

Many of our emotions are felt in the body - and sometimes only in the body, so it is important to be able to spot them and understand where they are coming from.

Fear might cause a tightness in the chest, or a tummy ache. Stress might give you a headache, or you may tense your muscles. Happiness is felt as a pleasant sensation in the entire body. Anger can cause a faster heart rate and you could clench your teeth and fists.

Scan your body and look for areas of heightened sensation. Rather than give it a label, you can stay with this sensation, and it gradually dissolves.

You can then explore where the sensation comes from and if it's linked to a feeling like stress or anxiety, or sadness.

Your emotions affect every cell in your body. Mind and body, physical and mental, are intertwined.

Thomas Tutko

Being sensitive to the sensations in your body can be an early sign of strong emotions you may be feeling. You can then explore these emotions and deal with them.
