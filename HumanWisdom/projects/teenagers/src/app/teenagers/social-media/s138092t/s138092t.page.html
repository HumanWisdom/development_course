There are many risks of browsing the internet and social media. Being aware of them can help you avoid them and deal with them better. There are risks of cyberbullying which impacts 1 in 3 teenagers. You should know your own boundaries and be able to exit a site or a conversation, or report it if needed. You may stumble upon sites that are inappropriate for teenagers - sites that promote self-harm, pornography, or gambling. Don’t be tempted to browse them. Exit them straight away as they can harm you. There are adults who may make friends with you and then try and meet you offline - their intentions should always be suspect - and you should never meet someone offline, that you met online - at least on your own. If you get an email from someone you don’t know with an attachment - don’t open it as it could contain a virus. There are other risks to be aware of. Read about them and be vigilant when online
