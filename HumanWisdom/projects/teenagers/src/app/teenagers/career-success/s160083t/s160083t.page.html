Communication is a critical skill to learn and the best way to begin is to practise it with your friends, teachers and family members.

Employers say many young people have not learned how to communicate with care. One manager told me how four young people sitting across a table were messaging each other rather than getting up and talking to each other.

Begin with being able to listen well, and not interrupt the person who is speaking. When they finish talking, be curious and ask a question rather than jumping in with your comments.

Before speaking, ask yourself how it is going to be received.

Try and understand what the other person is feeling, beyond the words being spoken.
